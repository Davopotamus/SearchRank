Updated Content



Introduction



The Google search engine sets algorithms to detect fresh new content. New content, especially news articles and blogs, will appear over older content of the same subject. Dead or outdated links have a negative impact on the overall performance of your listing and in time could raise flags if information is deemed inaccurate or false. If your content remains new and engaging, more users will continue to rely on your information, in return increasing your sites popularity.

Search Queries

Google checks the "freshness" of your sites content. With this knowledge, they determine if the freshness is relevant in terms of the search query. The importance of a baked potato recipe is less than that of an article about Covid-19. A baked potato will always bake with the same set of instructions. With the increasing amount of concern about the corona virus, it's important for Google to be aware of what is fresh, and what is false. If Google didn't have set algorithms in place, users would likely find an alternate method of finding information, as their results would be less than resourceful.

Small Changes

While you may think about going in and changing a sentence here or there to achieve fresh content, this approach may have a different outcome than expected. In some cases, Google has choosen to ignore pages that make minor tweaks and changes constantly without having content to back it up, like an article or post. When making minor changes, it helps to update all of the content surrounding the change. This gives the crawler bots an increased chance to scope your content earlier than expected due to your sites ability to keep things relevant.

Coding Content

One thing to note about Google algorithms is that changes to your CSS or Javascript will likely have less of an impact in the grading of your site. Google can recognize these changes but mainly uses it to determine the visibility of your content on devices and how it affects different browsers in terms of readability and useability. Sidebar or navigation links will also have little impact on bot crawlers picking up on major changes. When making changes, make sure it is within the body of the document with substantial differences from the original version.

New Pages

When updating content, a new page will rank or score higher than an existing unedited page. Frequently updated pages can score higher than a new page if your content is stale and hardly updated. Many factors contribute from all of the previous paragraphs when determining how page content is labeled in the form of fresh. Be sure to know when you need to make a new page or update outdated content. Often it is better to make a new page and reference the old page when blogging or updating how-to content.
